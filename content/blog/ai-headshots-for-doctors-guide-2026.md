## Why doctors need a current headshot

### Trust at first glance

A dated or casual selfie on Healthgrades or your practice site can undermine confidence. A consistent, well-lit portrait signals competence and approachability. In competitive markets, patients often compare several physicians online before booking — photo and bio quality are common tiebreakers.

### First impressions on directory pages

Prospective patients typically skim profile pages quickly before deciding to read more or move on. Your headshot is the anchor element in that skim — it should match how you present in clinic.

### Directory and marketing consistency

Many practices use the same physician photo across:

- Clinic website team pages
- Google Business Profile
- Healthgrades, Zocdoc, and hospital directories
- LinkedIn and conference speaker bios
- Press releases and local media features
- Telehealth platform profiles
- Insurance provider "Find a Doctor" tools

AI headshots help you refresh all of these from one upload session.

## How to get a strong doctor headshot with AI

### What to wear

- **White coat or business attire** — match what patients see in clinic
- **Solid colors**; avoid busy patterns on camera
- **Minimal jewelry**; no lanyards or ID badges in frame
- **Stethoscope**: not required; if you include it, keep it consistent with your clinical specialty

### How to shoot your selfies

1. **Upload 10–15 photos** from slightly different angles
2. **Use even, natural light** facing a window — avoid overhead fluorescent light
3. **Neutral expression**; eyes to camera
4. **Include shoulders-up and some waist-up frames**
5. **No group shots, sunglasses, or heavy filters**
6. **Plain backgrounds** in the input photos work best — busy home backgrounds confuse AI

### Styles that work on medical sites

- **Neutral gray or soft blue backgrounds** — classic, trustworthy
- **Warm but professional expression** — slight smile reads as approachable
- **Consistent lighting** across all deliverables
- For pediatricians: slightly brighter, warmer tones
- For surgeons: more contrast, sharper lighting

### Specialty-specific style guide

- **Primary care / family medicine**: warm, friendly, neutral background
- **Pediatrics**: brighter lighting, soft color tones
- **Surgery / specialist**: editorial lighting, more formal posture
- **Psychiatry / mental health**: softer tones, less clinical
- **Dentistry**: clean white or soft pastel backgrounds

## Frequently asked questions

### Are AI headshots allowed on medical websites?

Many private practices use them successfully; hospitals may require approval. Check your policy.

### Should I wear my white coat?

If that is how patients know you in clinic, yes — consistency matters more than the specific garment.

### How often should physicians refresh headshots?

Every **2–3 years**, or after major appearance changes, role changes, or rebranding.

### Are AI headshots HIPAA-compliant?

The headshot itself is not PHI. The vendor relationship is the consideration — most AI headshot tools are not Business Associates under HIPAA. Do not upload any images that contain patient PHI or identifiable backgrounds.

### Will patients be able to tell it's AI?

For a standard professional headshot with neutral background, most viewers cannot distinguish AI from studio. The relevant question is likeness fidelity — does the photo look like you on a good day, in person? Good AI tools, used with high-quality input selfies, deliver that.

### Should telehealth photos be different from clinic photos?

Telehealth profiles are typically tighter crops and slightly more relaxed expressions. Generate both from the same AI batch and crop differently.

### Can residents and fellows use AI headshots?

Yes, but check your training program's policy. Some programs require their own official portrait for departmental use; AI is appropriate for personal branding (LinkedIn, conference bios, fellowship applications).
