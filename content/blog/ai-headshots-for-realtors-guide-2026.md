## Why your headshot drives listings and leads

### Buyers choose people, not just properties

A warm, professional portrait on your profile suggests you are accessible, organized, and successful enough to represent their largest asset. According to the [National Association of Realtors 2024 Member Profile](https://www.nar.realtor/research-and-statistics/research-reports/guide-to-realtor-credentials-and-designations), 89% of buyers used a real estate agent in 2023 — and the first impression of that agent's photo heavily influences the contact decision.

### The science of trust in profile photos

Research from the [American Psychological Association](https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals) on facial perception shows that viewers form trust judgments from a photo in under 100 milliseconds. For realtors, that means your Zillow thumbnail needs to convey warmth and competence before a buyer even reads your bio.

### Every channel repeats your face

- Zillow and Realtor.com agent profiles
- Brokerage website bio
- Business cards and yard signs (print resolution matters)
- Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube thumbnails
- Email newsletters and Just Sold postcards
- Realtor.com review listings

Consistency across channels builds recognition in your farm area. A unified look — same background, similar lighting, comparable crop — signals professionalism every time a prospect sees you.

## What makes a great realtor headshot

### Approachable and professional

- **Genuine smile or confident neutral expression** — research published by [Harvard Business Review](https://hbr.org/) suggests authentic warmth outperforms posed formality for service professionals
- **Business casual or blazer** — match your market (luxury vs. suburban)
- **Clean background** — outdoor soft blur, neutral studio, or bright office

### Composition rules that work

- Crop from mid-chest up, leaving headroom
- Eyes in the upper third of the frame
- Shoulders angled 10–15 degrees to camera (avoids the "ID photo" feel)
- Soft, even lighting — no harsh shadows under eyes or nose

### Avoid these mistakes

- **Sunglasses or hats** — buyers want to see your eyes
- **Busy backgrounds** (listed living rooms can date quickly)
- **Over-retouched "glamour" looks** that feel inauthentic
- **Low-resolution crops from wedding photos** — pixelation on a Zillow profile reads as amateur
- **Group photo crops** — you alone in the frame, no cropping a friend out

### Regional positioning

Luxury agents may prefer darker, editorial styling. Volume agents often win with bright, friendly portraits. Midwest suburban markets favor classic, bright, friendly.

## How to create AI realtor headshots

### Upload checklist

1. **12–15 selfies** outdoors and indoors
2. **Golden-hour outdoor light** works well for lifestyle brands
3. Include one photo in **brokerage colors** if you wear them often
4. **Face the camera**; shoulders angled slightly
5. Mix of shoulder-up and chest-up frames
6. No sunglasses, no group shots, no heavy filters
7. Vary expressions: one smiling, one neutral, one "thinking"

### Picking your final images

- **One primary headshot** for MLS and Zillow (bright, smiling, square crop)
- **One alternate** for social (slightly wider crop, more relaxed)
- **One editorial variant** for luxury listings or print
- **Optional black-and-white** for print marketing

## FAQ

### Will AI headshots work on yard signs?

Yes if you export high-resolution files; always run a print proof. We recommend 300 DPI at final print size to avoid any pixelation on close inspection.

### How often should realtors update headshots?

At least every **18–24 months**, or after brokerage rebrand, major hairstyle change, or weight change. NAR [research on agent branding](https://www.nar.realtor/) suggests fresh photography correlates with higher contact rates.

### Can teams get matching backgrounds?

Yes — use the same vendor preset and attire brief for every agent. Consistency across team members is a strong differentiator for boutique brokerages.

### Will buyers notice it's an AI headshot?

For professional-looking profile photos with neutral backgrounds, most buyers cannot tell. The signal that matters — polished, warm, professional — comes through clearly.

### Should I include a white-coat equivalent for real estate?

Real estate has no "uniform," but business casual is the closest analog. Skip the suit if your market is casual suburban; lean blazer for luxury.

### What if my brokerage has a specific photo requirement?

Check your brokerage compliance guide first. Some franchises require photographer-vetted files. AI is usually fine for personal branding, not for franchise-mandated official portrait.
