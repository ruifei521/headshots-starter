## Frequently asked questions

### Is Dreamwave really that much better on privacy?

Yes — and we're comfortable acknowledging that. Dreamwave's combination of no-training-on-data, automatic 30-day deletion, data residency in the U.S., and no PII collection (not even your name) is the most comprehensive privacy framework among AI headshot tools. SnapProHead's privacy posture is solid (delete on request, no training, no sharing) but doesn't match Dreamwave's automatic deletion and no-name-collection policies. For regulated industries, this difference matters.

### Why doesn't Dreamwave offer refunds when SnapProHead does?

Dreamwave's stated reason is that AI headshot generation requires expensive, specialized hardware, and costs are incurred immediately upon starting generation. This is a legitimate operational explanation. SnapProHead's view is that user satisfaction is worth the cost of absorbing occasional refunds — it builds trust, especially for a newer brand. Both positions are defensible; the question is which one aligns with your risk tolerance.

### At Dreamwave's sale pricing, should I just buy Dreamwave?

If photo quantity, style variety, or privacy are your top priorities — and you're willing to accept the no-refund risk — then yes, Dreamwave's sale pricing ($35 for 75 photos) is compelling. If purchase protection matters to you, or if you're buying at regular pricing ($55+), SnapProHead's $29 entry with a satisfaction guarantee is the lower-risk option. The sale-vs-regular pricing gap at Dreamwave also means timing matters — buying during a non-sale period changes the value equation significantly.

### Can I get human-edited headshots from SnapProHead?

No. SnapProHead is an AI-only product. All our headshots are generated by Flux-based AI with no human touch-up layer. Dreamwave's Best Value tier ($99) includes unlimited human editing, which may produce more polished results — particularly for users with specific requests about skin, lighting, or framing. If human editing matters to you, Dreamwave is the only option in this comparison that offers it.

### Which tool works better for LinkedIn headshots specifically?

Both tools are well-suited for LinkedIn headshots. SnapProHead has a [dedicated LinkedIn headshots page](/headshots/linkedin) with examples tuned for that specific use case. Dreamwave's broader style catalog gives you more variety to find the exact LinkedIn-appropriate look you want. In practice, both will produce professional, platform-appropriate results. The tiebreaker is budget and risk tolerance.

### How many selfies should I upload to each tool?

- **SnapProHead:** 8–15 recommended. More variety in angles, lighting, and backgrounds = better results.
- **Dreamwave:** 5 minimum, 7–8 recommended. Unedited, unfiltered photos with varied clothing and backgrounds required. Dreamwave is stricter about input quality — photos that don't meet their guidelines may be rejected.

### Is Dreamwave's free tier worth trying?

Dreamwave offers a free AI headshot generator with limited daily spots. However, user reports suggest spots "fill within a minute or two," and the free tier operates on a lottery-like availability model. The output quality is also unclear — free tiers often use lower-resolution models or have watermarks. For $29–$35, a paid entry tier from either tool eliminates the uncertainty and waiting.
