## How we tested these tools

We did not rely on marketing screenshots alone. For each vendor below, we used the same evaluation checklist:

1. **Signup and upload** — How many selfies are required, and how strict is the quality gate?
2. **Time to first usable image** — Wall-clock minutes from payment to download-ready files.
3. **Likeness and consistency** — Do results look like one person across backgrounds?
4. **Pack economics** — Entry price divided by number of delivered photos (not promised "styles").
5. **Privacy language** — Retention period, training opt-out, and deletion policy in plain English.
6. **Refund path** — Whether a satisfaction or redo policy exists for bad batches.

We tested with the same set of **12 neutral selfies** (indoor window light, no filters, shoulders-up and waist-up). Scores below are editorial judgments for **solo professionals** using photos on LinkedIn or a company site — not enterprise procurement at 500 seats.

## What makes a good AI headshot generator?

Before comparing brands, here's what actually matters:

1. **Likeness** — You should still look like you, not a stock model.
2. **Consistency** — Lighting, skin tone, and framing should feel like one professional shoot.
3. **Use-case fit** — [LinkedIn](/headshots/linkedin), [law firm bio](/headshots/lawyer), or [corporate team page](/headshots/corporate) need different styles.
4. **Privacy** — Clear policies on how long uploads are stored and who can access them.
5. **Total cost** — Include retries, upsells, and whether you need multiple packs.

## Frequently asked questions

### Which AI headshot generator is cheapest in 2026?

For **solo users**, compare entry price **and** how many downloadable photos you get. SnapProHead's Starter tier is built around a low entry point with **40+ images**; Aragon and BetterPic often cost more per usable photo at the base tier. Teams should model per-seat cost separately.

### Are AI headshots good enough for LinkedIn?

Yes, when likeness is strong and lighting looks consistent. Use our [LinkedIn headshot guide](/headshots/linkedin) for framing and background tips. Avoid over-retouched "beauty filter" looks — recruiters notice.

### How long should I wait for results?

Consumer tools range from **under 30 minutes** to **24 hours**. If you have a deadline, pick a vendor that states queue time on the pricing page, not only in ads.

### Is my data used to train other people's models?

Policies differ. Read each vendor's terms for **training opt-out**, retention, and deletion. We publish a **30-day auto-delete** posture for generated assets; always verify the current policy at checkout.

### Can I use AI headshots on a law firm or medical website?

Many professionals do, with firm approval. See [lawyer](/headshots/lawyer) and [doctor](/headshots/doctor) pages for style notes; some firms still require a traditional photographer for partner bios.

## How to get better results (any tool)

Whichever generator you choose:

- Upload **8–15 clear selfies** from different days
- Face the camera, neutral expression, even lighting
- Avoid sunglasses, hats, heavy filters, or group shots
- Include **shoulders-up** and some waist-up frames
- Pick one primary use case (e.g. LinkedIn) before generating 100 random styles

## Free AI headshot generators — worth it?

Many "free" tools exist. In practice they often:

- Watermark images
- Limit resolution
- Use your photos for training without clear opt-out
- Upsell aggressively after a "free" trial
